Basic Nutrition

Q: How much of a nutrient is too much?
Nutrients are essential for health, but too much of certain vitamins and minerals can actually be harmful. This is known as nutrient toxicity. Each nutrient has a Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) (the daily amount you need for good health) and a Tolerable Upper Intake Level (UL) (the maximum daily amount unlikely to cause harm). For example: Vitamin C: RDA ~75–90 mg/day, UL = 2,000 mg/day. Too much may cause diarrhea or stomach upset. Vitamin D: RDA ~600 IU/day, UL = 4,000 IU/day. Excess may cause calcium buildup in blood. Iron: RDA 8–18 mg/day (varies), UL = 45 mg/day. Too much can damage organs. Getting too many nutrients usually happens from supplements, not from food.
Q: What is a "healthy diet"?
A healthy diet includes a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. It balances energy intake with activity levels to support long-term health.

Diet and Health Conditions

Q: Is there a high blood pressure diet?
Yes. The DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet is recommended for lowering blood pressure. It emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-sodium foods.
Q: What foods cause gas and bloating?
Foods such as beans, lentils, broccoli, cabbage, onions, and carbonated drinks can cause gas and bloating. Eating slowly and reducing carbonated beverages may help.
Q: Why is protein important in our diet?
Protein is essential for building and repairing tissues, including muscles, skin, and organs. It also supports immune function, produces enzymes and hormones, and provides energy when needed.

Food Safety

Q: Where can I view current food recalls?
Current food recalls can be found on government food safety websites, such as the USDA and FDA portals. These provide updated lists of recalled products and reasons for recall.
Q: How long can I keep leftover food?
Most leftovers can be stored safely in the refrigerator for 3–4 days. Freeze items for longer storage, and always reheat to safe internal temperatures.
Q: What foods can be canned at home?
Vegetables, fruits, jams, and pickles can be canned at home. Pressure canning is recommended for low-acid foods to prevent bacterial growth.

Dietary Supplements & Nutrition

Q: Are natural supplements good for you?
While some natural supplements can provide benefits, not all are safe. Consult with a healthcare provider before starting supplements, as they may interact with medications.
Q: Do I need a vitamin or mineral supplement?
Most people can get enough vitamins and minerals through a balanced diet. Supplements may be necessary if you have deficiencies or dietary restrictions.
Q: What are the risks and side effects of taking supplements?
Overuse of supplements may cause toxicity, organ damage, or other health issues. Always follow recommended dosages and avoid self-prescribing without guidance.
